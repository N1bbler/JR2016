About Me

Artist | Musician | Designer | Geek

I'm originally from San Luis Obispo and later the Monterey and Santa Cruz areas of California. I've lived in the Silicon Valley now for about ten years. I have had a talent for and love of creating art and music from a very young age. This has expressed itself in many ways, from drawing and painting to sculpture to writing (made 'zines in high school and was published in a poetry book at the age of 21), and with music (drums mainly, and a bit of guitar. I'm a former GSL and SubPop recording artist).

In my downtime I love spending time with family and friends and feel we live in the perfect part of San Jose, nestled in between Campbell and Los Gatos (in the Cambrian area) with good schools and close access to work. We regularly take short trips to Santa Cruz or Half Moon Bay when we need time at the beach, or short jaunts up to SF for all the City has to offer on the weekends.

In recent years I've developed a passion for collecting exotic succulent and cactus plants from around the world. I have an interest in plant identification and endeavor to memorize all the botanical names. Yes, it's nerdy, but I luckily have the good sense to not bore people talking about it, or at least when I can tell they aren't interested (haha)!

Thanks again for visiting my site. I hope you found it to be an enjoyable experience and liked what you saw. I'm ready to discover what new opportunities await on the horizon. Let's talk!

About This Site

In recent years, when I wanted to build a new site, I would design and prototype it in HTML and then manually port it into a Drupal or Wordpress template. There's lot's of reasons and advantages for running a site on a CMS. There are tradeoffs though, and frequent maintenace and security updates must be applied.

So when I set out to build my new portfolio site in late 2015, I wanted to try a new approach. I have been studying and reading up on Google's Material Design Specification, and investigating Polymer + Paper Elements, and Web Components technology. In many ways I do believe Web Components are the future of the Web. However, there are still deep browser disparities with how certain Web Component technologies are handled (such as with Firefox and HTML imports support, not to utter a word about IE), but Polymer certainly seems to do a fine job at bridging that gap. I've experimented a bit with the .5 beta as well as the new version 1.0 release. So I initially thought I might take this approach, and use the chance to learn something new.

But then I stumbled upon a CSS framework called Materialize which is based on the Material Design spec. Now generally, I'm not the hugest fan of CSS frameworks. For one, I can get hung up on the lack of good semantics. For another, I don't need them to display things exactly as I want them, I'm good with CSS 3. But, they are widely used with development teams and it makes a lot of sense to know them from that perspective. It defines a safe framework that non-experts can play in. It's a way to do what you need to do, maybe even elegantly, and quickly. Time to market matters (even with personal projects).

So, I decided to download it and give it a try. It was fun and easy to pickup. I realized that jQuery was integrated, so I got the chance to work with the newest version, it had been awhile. Materialize also leverages Web Fonts, which I was familiar with but hadn't actually worked with much before. Even though I love making my own icons, it was fun to learn a new, lightweight way to display icons (or glyphs, really). I then expanded the set with the Socicon web font. I found it enjoyable and easy to make my own styled social networking icons with it.

My site logo and favicon are an homage to Adobe Creative Suite. Finally, I hand-coded the site using Brackets editor from Adobe. I'm picky with my text-editors, but I like this quite a bit, after enabling a few add-on plugins. I've been developing locally running a XAMPP server, and I plan to publish on Oct. 12, 2015. I hope you enjoy the site, and we get a chance to speak soon!

~ Jason
