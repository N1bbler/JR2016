Case Study

Project Profile

Type: Site Redesign

Category: Consumer-Facing Website

Description: The Channel Cat Charters website is the online presence for the Channel Cat ship located in Santa Barbara Harbor, SB, CA. The site is used as a sales and marketing tool, as well as to demonstrate the qualities and features of the Channel Cat charter experience to potential clientele. The business caters to a high-end audience and offers individualized and highly personalized service. The (now former) site was launched in 2007. While it served them well for many years, it was built with legacy technology (Flash, Java applets) and was no longer working for them or many of their clients. This resulted in lost booking opportunities, as well as having to guide clients through the site, which was expensive in terms of time allocation. Previously, the site did not include any analytics solution, so measuring these lost opportunities was impossible.

Purpose of the site:

  • For clientele to see the beauty, size, and overall grandeur of the ship and then to contact the Charter Agent for a booking
  • For Charter Agent and other stakeholders to use the site as a sales and marketing tool

Goals for the redesign:

  1. Make Virtual Tours work across devices, the tours are a big selling point
  2. Make Video interview with Extreme Yachts television show work across devices
  3. Update the Directions to be more clear, requires custom map routes as the mailing address is not the same as the boarding location
  4. Make it easier for Users to navigate the content itself (not in tiny window frame)

Problems to Solve:

  • Site too small on modern monitors, difficult to navigate as content is shown in tiny window
  • Home page animation broken on mobile (Flash-based)
  • Virtual Tours broken on mobile, or in some Desktop browsers (Java-applet based)
  • Video tour broken on mobile (Flash-based)
  • Gallery broken on mobile (Flash-based), difficult to navigate
  • Site required walk-through guidance to use
  • Directions need to be reworked, but cannot use physical address

Technical Challenges:

  • Most Virtual Tour software tends to be Java based, produce Java applet output
  • Video content is embedded in Flash, only source available

Preliminary Client notes:

  • Wants Big beautiful pictures
  • Believes "Less is more" with marketing verbiage, wants pictures to speak for themselves
  • Client is a Senior Citizen that struggles a bit with computers and technology; ease of use, large images and scalable text is key
  • Client is not interested in going the route of a booking and reservation system; as a high-end chartering business with an exclusive clientele, they hold the customer's hands through every step of the booking process.
  • Client can send images via email, but many initial images sent are low-resoluton thumbnail images

My UX + UI Design Process

A detailed case-study follows of my design and development process of the site redesign. I explain each step along the way of a very iterative process and provide in-progress artifact screenshots, as well as a gallery at the end of the document showcasing the final result in before/after format.

Discovery, Research, Wireframes

  • Initial client consultation in order to:
    • Understand the business itself and purpose of the site
    • Understand the problems they are facing with the site
    • Understand their goals for the site redesign
    • Understand their clientele, as well as their goals and needs
    • Understand their internal use cases for the site
    • Establish delivery of content and assets
    • Establish any parameters or limitations
    • Estimate time to completion, explain process, and address administrative matters
  • Performed Site Audit to understand current site, content, technical implementation and to reinforce goals
  • Analyzed current workflow and information architecture
  • Performed Competitive Analysis in order to:
    • Be aware of competing Charter companies in Santa Barbara
    • Evaluate the pros and cons of competitive sites
    • Understand where the bar is set, in order to be sure we overshoot it
  • Reviewed current and new site content for awareness and inspiration
  • Defined initial User Profiles / Personas, as well as simple Use Cases
  • Engage in initial ideation and created low-fidelity wireframes (analog) to quickly define potential solutions and approach
  • Modelled different Call-To-Action (CTA) approaches
  • Define and explain thoughts on Navigational Model changes (old site to new, responsive site)

Initial User Personas

The first User Personas did capture some aspects of actual users, but were in need of refinement. I learned the average age was much older than initially assumed, and that online reservations where less of a priority both for the business and for their clientele.

Low-Fidelity Wireframes

Quick, rough sketches were used to begin mapping out the Home page layout. This generated new ideas as well as questions for the client.

Lo-Fi Gallery

Low-fidelity sketches of two Gallery interaction concepts. Note these in no way reflect my drawing skill; the purpose is to rapidly capture ideas, not to produce a beautiful drawing to hang on the wall.

Communicate, Validate, Educate

  • Follow up client consultation in order to:
    • Validate findings from Competitive Analysis (Client agreed with findings)
    • Validate initial User Personas and Use Cases (they needed refining, these are rarely exact on the first attempt)
    • Validate initial wireframe concepts, walk them through and explain wireframes are like blueprints
    • Walked client through process of locating original files stored in Picassa which they use to manage photos, and how to determine file size and resolution of images via Windows Explorer
    • Obtained high-resolution photos and discussed content strategy with client
    • Discussed proposed navigational model with client
    • Explained concept of Call-To-Action and plan to have every page guide the User to Contact them, which ultimately is the primary goal of the site

I updated the User Personas document, to reflect increased understanding of the site's user base.

  • Due to older demographic, it is particularly important to ensure text is large and readable
  • Simple and easy to use navigation is key
  • More clients search on mobile now than the past, so a responsive design is important
  • Conveying ship capacity is very important for planning large events
  • Large, beautiful pictures, as well as virtual tours and videos help sell the experience
  • Since online booking is not a possibility, make it as easy as possible to contact Charter agent

Updated User Personas


Updated User Personas reflected revised age estimates. Although the changes were minimal, it is important to keep the personas updated based on current understanding and lessons learned.

Process Feedback, Med-Fi Wireframes

  • Based on feedback from client, I learned:
    • Client does not want to use old logo, yet feels there is not a need for a new one at this time
    • It is important to the client to not crop the sail off of (at least the majority) of the images
    • Client wants full-screen images on the homepage, without any overlay text.
    • Client *loved* the idea of a slideshow (or carousel) of selected images on the homepage
    • Client likes the new/improved navigational model proposed
    • Client likes the proposed Call-To-Action model, is OK with it being "below the fold" on the homepage

With this new feedback, I was ready to move onto digital, medium-fidelity wireframes (created in Sketch app), in order to present these to the client. I created these quickly from the low-fi mockups as a reference, along with my ample client notes and client feedback documentation.

Communicate, Validate, Iterate

  • Based on the discussion with the client regarding the medium-fidelity wireframes, it was determined:
    • In Desktop HD mode we would right-align the navigation
    • Client wanted 7-10 images instead of 3 in Carousel
    • In Tablet Portrait view we would switch to mobile navigation mode
  • Initial color-palette suggestions were also approved:
    • White reflected the ship itself, and lended clean, modern feel to the site with lots of "white space"
    • Sky-Blue reflected clear waters, a much lighter blue accent color than was used previously
    • Complementary tints and shades would be used for interaction
    • We would drop the green color from the mobile menu, as it distracts
    • Overall, the limited color palette was designed to direct focus to the content itself

Color Palette


Designed a new, limited color palette with the intention of keeping the site light and modern feeling, in order to allow the content itself to be the focus.

Medium-Fi Wireframes


Medium-fidelity wireframes of the homepage where more appropriate to show this particular client, and allowed me to demonstrate how the layout would change on different devices with responsive design.

Technology Stack / Tools Used

  • Based on the now known site functionality, user and business goals and needs, as well as the wireframes feedback, it was time to move on to rapid prototyping, and so I researched and decided on the following technology choices:
    • Bootstrap 3: for the mobile-first responsive UI capabilities
    • jQuery: for the carousel functionality, extensive examples, and plugins available
    • jQuery/Bootstrap Responsive Tabs: exactly what I needed to handle inline tab content in a responsive way
    • Gridder 1.4.1: a jQuery based library which was exactly what I was looking for the Gallery functionality
    • Font Awesome: for WebFont icons, used in Gridder/Gallery UI for next, back and close buttons.
    • Pano2VR: modern vitural tour software capable of exporting to HTML5 , CSS3, XML, and WebGL, which works on modern devices and platforms.
    • XAMPP: a simple webserver-in-a-box in order to develop the site locally, prior to uploading to the remote test site (with Filezilla for SFTP).
    • Brackets: my current preferred text editor, which is all I need to code HTML5, CSS3, and JavaScript with.
    • Adobe Photoshop CC®, Adobe Illustrator CC®: for image formatting, optimization, and minor map topography overlay work.
    • Google Analytics: In order to measure/learn from user behavior in the future (no analytics were used on the site in the past).

Git Version Control


The command line tool Git + Github was used for version control and repository management.

Initial Prototypes

  • Using Adobe Photoshop CS, I readied all images and optimized them for the Web
  • Using Bootstrap, I quickly built the initial home page prototypes, including the Header area and navigation and basic layout
  • Using jQuery, I built the Carousel prototype on the home page
  • Using Pano2VR, I built the Virtual Tours and using inline tabs in Bootstrap, laid them out with the grid
  • Using a jQuery script, I implemented responsive tabs design pattern for the virtual tours to ensure a good experience on mobile
  • Using Gridder, I built the Gallery prototypes and added FontAwesome UI controls
  • Using custom HTML and CSS, as well Bootstrap helper classes, I laid out the About prototype in a magazine-inspired layout
  • Using custom HTML and CSS, I laid out the Contact Page
  • Researching and then leveraging the Google Maps API, I added an interactive map to the directions prototype, and used screenshots + Adobe Illustrator CS to add custom routes
  • Using Google's Developer Tools for debugging and early optimization to target any performance bottlenecks
  • Using GIT and GitHub, I backed up all of my work with version control. See the commit history here.

Communicate, Validate, Negotiate

  • Follow up client consultation in order to:
    • Validate Home page design (we decided to go back to full-height carousel and move Virtual Tours to their own page)
    • Validate Gallery design (clients loved this as is, but had extra photos they wanted to add)
    • Validate Virtual Tours design (client was excited at this, I showed it working on mobile as well. We also discussed adding the video to this page)
    • Validate About design (client also loved this but wanted to re-use photo from home page. After discussing my reasoning for using the particular panoramic photo, they agreed with this and we decided to proceed as is)
    • Validate Directions design (client wasn't sure about the interactive map, but when I discussed the idea behind it, we agreed to keep it but to move it below the directions themselves as a secondary feature)

Iteration


  • With the full green-light now in place, I worked quickly to expand and refine the initial work, utilizing all feedback to date:
    • Refined navigation with expanding text for mobile mode
    • Moved the Virtual Tours to their own page and added Extreme Yachts video after extracting it from previous Flash source and converting it to use modern web codecs, which allowed for native HTML5 playback
    • Added tons of content to the Gallery, moved images around and randomized things a bit for a more interesting presentation
    • Added remainder of content to the About page and worked heavily on the typography for this text-heavy page, utilizing smart quotes and responsive text, as well as numerous stylistic type treatments
    • Built the Contact page around the Call-to-Action design which is at the bottom of each page on the site. Also added full-screen background of the crew to also convey to the user they are in the right place

Communicate, Review, Refine

I continued working until the site was close to complete. At this point, I reviewed with the client again, and they were ecstatic. They were ready to launch the new site immediately. I explained there was some work to be done to optimize the site for best performance. Additionally, I suggested that we implement Google Analytics in order to measure and learn from the site's users going forward. Finally, although cross-browser testing and mobile tests had occurred throughout the design and development cycles, I wanted to do one last round of thorough checking and capture screenshots as well.

Final Phase


  • Implemented Google Analytics across all pages on the site
  • General code cleanup and formatting
  • Double-checked to ensure all images had been optimized
  • Minified all CSS used on the site and relinked to new minified files
  • Extensive cross-platform and cross-browser testing on desktop; Windows: FF, Chrome, and IE; Mac OS: Safari, Chrome, and Firefox
  • One minor display bug was found on Safari for IOS, which I was able to quickly fix with some custom CSS
  • Mobile testing on Android (Chrome), no issues noted
  • Committed final code edits to Github repo
  • Backed up and then took down the old site, as well as the test/ remote staging site, and replaced with the new site
  • Performed another round of testing on the live site to ensure all was well
  • Ran into a couple of unexpected issues, which turned out to be merely file permissions related. CHMOD to the rescue.
  • Checked Google Analytics statistics to ensure all was working (24 hours for complete stats)

Postscript: Retrospective and Prospective

What went well? Business problems solved. User needs addressed. Technical challenges conquered. Analytics added. Desktop and Mobile testing successful. Project completed on time and on budget. Results: Immense client satisfaction!

What didn't go so well? Wait times for images and verbiage content from client were at times lengthy. Some low-resolution content (video, virtual tours, a couple of the gallery images) had to be used as no higher-quality content available.

What would I do differently if starting over? Establish firmer deadlines to receive content and follow up on it. Ensure all content is of sufficient quality and ready to go beforehand.

How well did the process work? Kanban was a good approach for this project, communication levels were good, iterations went smoothly; overall process was effective with very positive results.

Next Steps:

  • Google Analytics data will be monitored to learn from users and to determine if any changes need to be considered
  • Video content will be re-exported from newly found DVD source, trimmed, and converted, before upgrading
  • Virtual tours will be revisited with higher-quality imagery if client can locate it (TBD)
